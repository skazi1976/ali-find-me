How it's calculated

1RM is estimated with the Epley formula: 1RM = weight × (1 + reps/30). It estimates the maximum weight you could lift once. The percentage table helps plan workouts (e.g., 80% for 5 reps).

Frequently asked questions

What is 1RM?

1RM (One-Rep Max) is the maximum weight you can lift for one rep of an exercise. It's used to plan strength-training intensity.

How do I know my 1RM without maxing out?

Lift a submaximal weight for several reps and the calculator estimates your 1RM — safer than a true max attempt.

How do I use the percentage table?

For muscle building work around 70-80% of 1RM (8-12 reps); for strength 85-95% (2-5 reps).
